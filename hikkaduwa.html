Hikkaduwa Turtle hatchery


If you're ever in Hikkaduwa, make a stop at the local sea turtle hatchery - it's a unique opportunity to get up close and personal with these gentle marine reptiles, while also learning how you can help protect these lovely creatures for future generations to enjoy!


Five different species of sea turtle visit the beaches of Sri Lanka, all of which are threatened with extinction due to habitat degradation and increasing human pressures. Establishments such as the Hikkaduwa Sea Turtle Hatchery work to counteract these challenges and provide these marine reptiles a fighting chance at survival. Turtle eggs are gathered and kept secure until they hatch, at which point the hatchlings are released back into the ocean, and old and damaged turtles are rehabilitated and provided shelter. During your visit, you may also view all of them, marveling as the elder turtles relax and strut around their tanks, and delighting as the baby turtles joyfully swim around their enclosures, yearning to get out into the wild.


If you visit the hatchery at the correct time, you may even be able to participate in the baby turtle release! Watch as these adorable young reptiles scurry away to the waiting ocean, the vast blue expanse where they'll spend the rest of their lives!



You can engage in a variety of activities and make your pet's life enjoyable. Basking, navigating obstacle courses, investigating their surroundings, excavating, playing with toys, and pursuing live prey like small fish are just a few of the activities that turtles like doing.
