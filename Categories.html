Turtle Categories

Of the 250 species of turtles on the planet, seven are marine turtles (also known as sea turtles), and the other 243 are terrestrial turtles (also known as tortoises) or dwell in freshwater ponds and marshes. The Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas), Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata), Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea), Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta), and Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) all nest on Sri Lankan beaches. Along with the Parker's Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga pakeri), the Sri Lanka Black Turtle (Melanochelys trijuga thermalis), and the Soft or Flapshell turtle (Lissemys punctata punctata), Sri Lanka is also home to one terrestrial tortoise species, the Star tortoise (Geochelone elegans).


Turtles are distinct animals known for their shells and are relatives to tortoises. These reptiles existed for hundreds of years before we arrived! Turtles can be found in water as well as on rocky terrain. According to scientific findings, the turtle population consists of approximately 300 different species. Except for Antarctica, turtles can be found on all continents. This article will look at the distinguishing qualities of many varieties of turtles.

    Green sea turleThe green sea turtle is the biggest sea turtle with a hard shell. Because they mostly feed seagrasses and algae, they stand out among sea turtles as herbivores. Their name derives from the fact that their fat is greenish in color, not from their shells.
    olive ridley turtle The olive ridley turtle, which is the most prevalent species of marine turtle in the world1, is well-known for its large nesting groups, or arribadas. They are also among the smallest sea turtle species, at about 2 to 2.5 feet (0.6 to 0.7 m) long and 80 to 110 pounds (36 to 50 kg).
    Leather black sea turtle The world's largest turtle is the leatherback sea turtle. They are the only species of sea turtle that don't have hard shells or scales. Since the time of the dinosaurs, they have existed in their current form and are known for their thick, rubbery skin.
