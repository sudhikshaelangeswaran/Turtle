Other turtle hatcheries


Sea turtles and other species can be found in Sri Lanka. On the western and southern shores of Sri Lanka, sea turtle hatcheries are frequently visible.When female sea turtles begin to migrate to their nesting areas to lay their eggs, this is the ideal time to visit a sea turtle hatchery.Turtles have long placed their eggs in Sri Lanka's southern shore.They enter the Indian Ocean by swimming there, then drag themselves up the sand on the beaches close to Galle, where they dig their nests and lay their eggs. Many nations, notably the southeast coast of Sri Lanka, have sea turtle hatcheries as an ex situ conservation technique. Sea turtle hatcheries have been credited with helping to save them, however questions have been raised about their effectiveness. By fusing economic and ecological criteria, Tisdell and Wilson analyzed the function of tourism-based sea turtle hatcheries and showed that, while hatcheries can contribute to sea turtle conservation, their efficacy depends on management.
